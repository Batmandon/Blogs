Right Join and Other Join Types

Summary

  • Right Joins: A Right Join returns all rows from the right table and the matching rows from the left table. It is functionally the mirror image of a Left Join. Because the same result can be achieved by flipping the table order in a Left Join, Right Joins are often unnecessary and less intuitive for many developers.
  • Full Outer Joins: A Full Outer Join returns every row from both tables, including matching rows and rows that exist only in one table. For example, joining users and posts would include valid user-post matches, users with no posts, and posts with no matching user. This can feel like combining three different datasets instead of producing a focused result.
  • Cross Joins: A Cross Join produces every combination of rows from both tables. It is mentioned as an advanced or esoteric join type and is not typically used in everyday SQL queries.
  • Key Takeaway: Inner Joins and Left Joins are the workhorses of SQL. They cover the vast majority of common use cases, while Right Joins and Full Outer Joins are useful only in specific scenarios.
